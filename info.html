The Paradox of a Divided Land

Despite Karnataka's rich crop diversity, its agricultural potential is constrained by the highly fragmented nature of its farm holdings. The vast majority of farmers operate on small, economically vulnerable plots.

Distribution of Farm Holdings

Over two-thirds of Karnataka's farms are classified as small or marginal, covering less than two hectares. This structure limits the adoption of modern technology and makes individual farmers highly susceptible to economic and environmental shocks.

Pathways to Responsible Deployment

Technical success is not enough. A sustainable and equitable system must be built on a strong ethical foundation that protects and empowers farmers.

Algorithmic Fairness

Mitigate bias by training on diverse datasets from all farm sizes and regions, ensuring recommendations are equitable.

Data Sovereignty

Empower farmers with ownership and control over their data through transparent policies and robust consent mechanisms.

Farmer Autonomy
